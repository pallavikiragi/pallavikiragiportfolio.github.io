Cyberculture in Airports

  • May 2023
  • Multimedia, Data visualizationTouchdesigner, AR/VR, UX Study

This project explores generative design through computational processes, creating diverse design options within the context of new media and airport operations. By examining cyberculture and stakeholder dynamics, it highlights how digital networks shape travel experiences. Research included site visits, case studies, interviews with passengers and employees, and firsthand observations of airport systems, complemented by insights from a Heathrow Airport documentary. These methods provided a comprehensive understanding of operational challenges, laying the foundation for innovative solutions in airport environments.

Objective

The project aims to merge new media creation with prototyping tools to develop micro-outputs addressing common airport challenges. Recognizing the learning curves in prototyping and system ideation, the approach focuses on Conceptual Prototyping—testing purposeful interactions within complex systems. This exploration reflects my philosophy of experimenting with redundant systems, using the complexity of airports as an opportunity for creative disruption. It also serves as preparation for industry readiness post-graduation. By integrating these methods, the project seeks to transform complexity into actionable, creative solutions. The process involves:

  • Research and Exploration: Investigating stages, stakeholders, and potential interventions to simplify operations.
  • Structured Timeline: A month-long exploratory study (March) followed by problem-solving and model creation (April).
  • Engagement and Testing: Conducting primary and secondary research, airport visits, passenger and employee interviews, prototyping, and mapping travel flows from multiple perspectives.

Narrowing Outputs

My aim was to rekindle a sense of wonder through technology, foster engagement with tools, and manage evolving expectations. Viewing the airport as the interface between passengers and airlines, I recognized that airline customers inherently become the airport's customers. I explored the innovative applications of new media in visual communication, focusing on visual expression through information technology (IT), flexible layouts, diverse transmission modes, and interactive integration. The Micro-Outputs of this project include AI widgets, Flight Tracker installation, Multimedia kiosks, and Hologram signage. Together, these elements embody cyberculture principles by leveraging the internet and data as technology, introducing creative and collaborative tools, shaping new social contexts, and serving as mediums of communication.

Concept Maps

Outcome 1: Flight Tracker Installation

A flight tracking installation uses a spherical Earth projection with markers to display live flight locations. Passengers can scan their boarding passes to access real-time flight details, including route, status, and gate information, enhanced with AR technology for interactive viewing. Projected on walls or floors, the installation is adjustable via external controls, such as a MIDI keyboard. By visualizing complex data, it optimizes operations, enhances passenger experiences, and fosters engagement, embodying cyberculture principles of using data as a creative and collaborative tool.

Outcome 2: Crowd Circles

A projected installation with physical poles and circular floor projections enhances crowd optimization in airports, reducing disorientation and anxiety during delays or disruptions. The poles serve as landmarks for queue management, while projected circles act as tangible boundaries, dynamically adjusted using a MIDI keyboard to guide passenger movement. Color-coded projections (red, yellow, green) indicate crowd density, ensuring passengers can reorient themselves and avoid congestion. By improving flow, monitoring occupancy, and creating organized spaces, this system fosters safety, efficiency, and comfort, providing a structured social context within tangible bounds.

Outcome 3: Air Widgets

Widgets enhance the travel experience by providing quick, convenient access to essential information and services. With user-friendly interfaces, passengers can easily receive real-time flight updates, gate changes, baggage details, and personalized notifications on their devices, ensuring seamless and informed decision-making. Interactive wayfinding widgets also offer guidance on airport amenities, dining, shopping, and time management, streamlining navigation for a hassle-free journey to your destination.

Key Learnings & Reflections

  • Exploration and Ambition: The project pushed me to explore new communication mediums and manage time effectively within a tight timeline. While ambitious, narrowing focus was challenging due to diverse opportunities and technologies.
  • Context and Direction: Confidently selecting new media and airports allowed experimentation with systems and cyberculture, though integrating outputs with context required substantial ideation and iteration.
  • Challenges and Growth: Conducting surveys and interviews onsite was outside my comfort zone but essential. Multiple prototypes were necessary to achieve project goals despite calls to simplify.
  • Technology and Testing: Exploring tools helped shape outputs, and testing validated learnability and proof of concept, creating a wholesome, impactful project.
Future Goal: I aim to create more functional prototypes and secure permissions to implement installations at airports. This project deepened my understanding of technology’s potential and its role in creating meaningful experiences, a perspective I intend to carry forward in future projects.
